// Deep Dives

Case studies.

A closer look at the engineering decisions, tradeoffs, and results behind two key projects.

Case Study

InsightTrader

Trading Journal & Analytics Platform
Problem

Existing trading journals were either too simple (spreadsheets) or too expensive and bloated. Traders needed a tool that could ingest data from any broker, reconstruct complex multi-leg trades, and provide genuine statistical insight — not just pretty charts.

12+
Broker Integrations
10K
Monte Carlo Sims
Solo
Full-Stack Build
Approach

Built a monorepo architecture with a Next.js 15 frontend and FastAPI backend. Designed a universal CSV parser that normalizes data across 12+ broker formats. Implemented a trade reconstruction engine that handles splits, merges, and partial fills. Added AI-powered analysis using both OpenAI and Anthropic for trade coaching.

Results

Live at insighttrader.io with active users and Stripe subscriptions. The Monte Carlo simulation engine processes 10,000 iterations in under 2 seconds. Users can import their entire trading history and get statistical analysis of their edge within minutes.

Case Study

Habify

AI-Powered Habit Tracker
Problem

Streak-based habit trackers punish users for missing a single day, destroying motivation. The psychology literature shows that momentum — not perfection — drives lasting behavior change. No app on the market implemented this insight.

Live
Google Play Store
AI
Coaching Engine
XP
Gamification System
Approach

Designed a physics-inspired momentum engine that calculates habit strength based on consistency, recency, and frequency — not binary streaks. Built with React Native + Expo for cross-platform, FastAPI backend with Supabase, and an AI coaching system using GPT-4 that adapts to each user's patterns.

Results

Published on the Google Play Store with monetization via RevenueCat. The momentum system reduced user churn from missed days by reframing progress. Gamification layer (XP, levels, badges) drives daily engagement. AI coach provides personalized suggestions based on completion history.
